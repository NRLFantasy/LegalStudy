Public Law

Public law governs the relationship between individuals and the state. It includes criminal, administrative, and constitutional law, ensuring social order and protection of rights.

Private Law (Civil Law)

Private law regulates disputes between individuals, including contract, tort, and property law. Remedies are designed to resolve conflicts and compensate affected parties.

Court Procedures

Criminal courts prosecute offences against society, while civil courts resolve disputes between parties. Both follow structured procedures to guarantee fairness and justice.

Legal Personnel

Judges, lawyers, and court staff play specific roles in facilitating the legal process. Their work ensures laws are applied consistently and disputes are resolved properly.

Common vs Civil Law Systems

Common law relies on judicial precedent and case law, while civil law is codified in statutes. Understanding both systems explains how legal principles are applied domestically and internationally.

Practice Questions

1. What is the difference between public and private law?

Public law deals with the state and society, while private law deals with disputes between individuals.

2. How does a common law system differ from a civil law system?

Common law relies on judicial decisions and precedent; civil law relies on codified legislation.
