Meaning of Law

Law is a system of rules enforced by institutions to maintain order and protect rights. It provides guidelines for acceptable behaviour and resolves disputes fairly.

Customs, Rules and Law

Customs are traditional social practices, rules are group-specific guidelines, and laws are enforceable norms applying to all citizens. Each plays a role in societal order.

Values and Ethics

Values represent what society considers important, and ethics are principles guiding moral behaviour. Laws often reflect both to maintain fairness and justice.

Characteristics of Just Laws

Just laws are clear, consistent, fair, and uphold human rights. They promote equality, fairness, and access to justice for all members of society.

Nature of Justice

Justice ensures fairness, equality, and access to legal remedies. Procedural fairness guarantees unbiased and transparent legal processes.

Procedural Fairness

Procedural fairness, or natural justice, ensures everyone has a fair hearing, the right to present evidence, and an unbiased decision-maker.

Rule of Law

The rule of law ensures that all members of society, including lawmakers, are subject to the law. It prevents arbitrary power and protects citizens’ rights.

Anarchy and Tyranny

Anarchy occurs when no laws exist, causing disorder. Tyranny occurs when power is abused, often ignoring fairness and rights. Laws prevent both extremes.

Practice Questions

1. Define law in your own words.

Law is a system of rules enforced by institutions to maintain order, protect rights, and resolve disputes fairly.

2. Explain the difference between customs, rules, and laws.

Customs are social traditions, rules are guidelines within groups, and laws are legally enforceable norms applied to society.
