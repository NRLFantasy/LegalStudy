Common Law

Originating from British law, common law is based on precedent, equity, and the adversarial trial system. Courts interpret and apply these laws consistently to ensure fairness.

Court Hierarchy

State and federal courts operate in a hierarchy, with higher courts able to overrule lower courts. This ensures uniformity in legal decisions and precedents.

Statute Law

Statute law consists of laws made by parliament. Delegated legislation is made by authorities under parliament’s power, and all statutes must follow legislative procedures.

Parliamentary Role

Parliament drafts, debates, and passes laws to reflect societal needs. Legislative procedures ensure laws are fair, clear, and enforceable.

Constitution

The Constitution outlines the separation and division of powers and defines the role of the High Court. It establishes Australia’s legal framework and guarantees rights.

Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Peoples’ Customary Laws

These laws are diverse, based on spirituality, family, and kinship. Some elements have been integrated into Australian law, influencing dispute resolution and justice practices.

International Law

International law governs states and organisations, with sources including treaties, customs, and UN declarations. Domestic law incorporates some international law depending on sovereignty.

Practice Questions

1. What is the role of common law?

Common law guides courts through precedent and ensures fairness in legal interpretation.

2. How does international law impact Australia?

International law influences domestic law when treaties or agreements are incorporated into Australian legislation.
